    • derivatives: rules
      Vectors
      A vector represents magnitude and direction.
      Dot product
      $$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}$$ $$\hat{i} \cdot \hat{i}=1$$ $$\hat{j} \cdot \hat{j}=1$$ $$\hat{i} \cdot \hat{j}=0$$
      Cross product
      $$\vec{a} \times \vec{b}$$ $$\hat{i} \times \hat{i}=\hat{j} \times \hat{j}=0$$ $$\hat{i} \rightarrow \hat{j} \rightarrow \hat{k}$$
      Right hand rule
      Curled fingers go towards arrow of cross product
      Out of board: $\circledcirc$
      Into board: $\otimes$
    • derivatives: calculus
      Derivatives & Integrals
      Do derivatives and integrals normally except do it with respect to their vectorial representaions.
      Arclength
      $\displaystyle \int ||\vec{v}||dt$
      Distance traveled
      $\displaystyle \int \sqrt{dx^2+dy^2} dt$
      Magnitude
      $||\vec{a}||=\sqrt{a \cdot a}$
